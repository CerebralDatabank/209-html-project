Silver Taps is a a tradition unique to Texas A&M and was held for the first time starting in 1898. It is held on the first Tuesday of each month to honor students at A&M who have passed away. The ceremonies are held from September to April at the Academic Plaza. The day of the ceremony, students can write letters to the families of the fallen Aggies. The ceremony starts that night at 10:15 pm, all lights on campus are required to be turned off. Families of the fallen and students gather silently in the Academic Plaza while hymns are played on the Albritton Bell Tower. At 10:30 pm, a three-volley salute is fired once the Ross Volunteer Company arrives. After the last round is fired, buglars begin to play "Silver Taps" while atop the Academic Building. It is played three times: once to the north, south, and west. However, never to the east because it is believed that "the sun will never rise on that Aggie again."
